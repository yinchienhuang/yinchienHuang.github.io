GraphRAG for System-of-Systems Integration

I lead research on GraphRAG, a framework combining large language models and knowledge graphs to tackle the complexity of System-of-Systems (SoS). By applying clustering and entropy-based evaluation, we provide a scalable method for real-time knowledge integration.

  • Contributions: Automated graph construction, semantic entity merging, hierarchical knowledge modeling
  • Use Case: Urban Air Mobility (UAM), supported by NASA S2A2
  • Tools: LLMs, community detection (Leiden algorithm), ontology filters

Model-Based Systems Engineering + AI

I investigate how LLM-powered knowledge graphs can support Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) through bidirectional linking with SysML components. This enhances requirement traceability and allows real-time adaptation based on evolving design or regulatory constraints.

  • Direct links between knowledge graph nodes and MBSE models
  • Integrated analysis of system requirements and external regulations
  • Visualization tools to bridge stakeholders with different technical backgrounds

Entropy Metrics for Complexity Analysis

I use Shannon entropy to measure complexity in system knowledge graphs, quantifying how clustering and semantic unification reduce uncertainty. This forms the basis for evaluating design modularity and SoS architecture scalability.

  • Flat vs. clustered vs. hierarchical entropy comparisons
  • Used in UAM to evaluate knowledge integration effectiveness
  • Supports adaptive abstraction and model transparency
